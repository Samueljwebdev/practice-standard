# Competitor Analysis: The Practice Standard
*Research date: May 2026*
*Prepared for: thepracticestandard.co.uk*

---

## Context

The Practice Standard is the UK's first job board built exclusively for regulated private healthcare practices — covering aesthetics, veterinary, optometry, physiotherapy, private GP, and dental in a single platform. Practices pay to post (£149/listing or £249/month Practice Pro); candidates always free.

This document profiles the primary competing platforms across three tiers: general boards (volume players), NHS-aligned boards (explicitly wrong market), and discipline-specific specialist boards. A final section identifies the strategic opportunities this landscape creates.

---

## 1. Indeed UK

**URL:** uk.indeed.com  
**Type:** General job board — the world's largest by volume

### Positioning
"Hire quickly with increased visibility." Targets every employer in every sector. No healthcare or private practice positioning. Default search surface for most UK job seekers regardless of sector.

### Pricing (as of 2026)
- **Free posts:** Up to 3 jobs/month at no cost. 30-day listing. Minimal visibility.
- **Standard Sponsored Jobs:** Pay-per-click model. From £1–£15 CPC depending on market and role. Since July 2025, minimum daily budget of £25 per job per day. Each job requires its own standalone budget — no pooling.
- **Premium Sponsored Jobs:** Higher CPC tier. Includes AI features, branded job listings, SMS candidate messaging, and "Matched Candidates." Pricing set dynamically.
- **Free credit for new accounts:** £100 sponsored job credit on signup (expires after one year).
- **Net cost in practice:** A modest 4-week healthcare listing on Standard Sponsored at £25/day minimum = £700. No cap guarantee on quality.

### Key Features
- Massive UK reach: 77.5% market share among active UK job seekers
- 250M+ monthly visitors globally; 600,000+ UK jobs at any time
- AI candidate matching (Premium tier)
- Screening questions, analytics dashboard, candidate messaging
- Free to post (with very low organic visibility)

### Weaknesses / Gaps vs The Practice Standard
- **No private practice filtering.** A dental associate role sits alongside McDonald's team member roles in candidate search results.
- **Catastrophically poor candidate quality for healthcare.** Multiple Trustpilot and employer reviews confirm that even with screening questions enabled, "unqualified persons are still able to apply." One employer called Indeed "a very poor site for obtaining skilled healthcare labour (veterinarians specifically)."
- **The 68% problem.** Industry data shows 68% of Indeed applicants fail initial screening for healthcare roles. Practices waste hours reviewing irrelevant CVs.
- **Minimum budget forcing escalating costs.** The July 2025 £25/day minimum per listing means a month of visibility on a single role costs £700+ — compared to £149 on The Practice Standard with a targeted audience.
- **No registration/verification.** Candidates self-describe with no credential check. Anyone can apply to any clinical role.
- **NHS-dominated candidate pool.** Healthcare professionals on Indeed are predominantly looking for NHS roles. Private practice visibility is buried.
- **3.3 stars on Trustpilot** from employers; frequent complaints about fake jobs, poor customer support, and irrelevant applicants flooding inboxes.

### SEO Strength
Dominant. Indeed.co.uk has one of the highest domain authorities of any UK website (DA 90+). They rank for effectively every job-related search term including "dental nurse jobs London," "veterinary surgeon jobs UK," and "physiotherapist private practice." The Practice Standard cannot compete head-on for generic terms — but can win on hyper-specific private practice intent queries where Indeed is generic noise.

### What Practices Complain About
- "Bombarded with applicants who are zero of a match to your job advert"
- "Even with screening questions turned on, Indeed still fills the inbox with lots of applicants who do not actually meet our requirements"
- "Virtually all applications are from completely unqualified individuals"
- "Wasted over £150 with no qualified candidate"
- Costs are opaque and escalating — the CPC model is unpredictable

## 2. Reed

**URL:** reed.co.uk  
**Type:** General UK job board — largest UK-native board

### Positioning
"Connecting employers with people quickly through the UK's most effective job advert and CV Search service." Targets all UK employers. Claims 26M+ registered candidates. No sector-specific focus. Backed by Reed, one of the UK's largest recruitment agencies.

### Pricing (as of 2026)
- **Pay-per-listing:** From £89 +VAT for a basic listing. Job live for 6 weeks.
- **Premium/featured listing:** Up to £250 +VAT per listing (standard); Reed's own comparison data shows priority at £399 and featured/SEO-optimised at £799+.
- **Subscription:** From £100/month — unlimited job postings + access to CV database (10.5M+ candidates claimed).
- **Pay-Per-Application model:** Post free, pay only when candidates click "apply." Budget-capped.
- **CV database access:** Separate purchase.

### Key Features
- 26M+ registered candidates (all sectors)
- 70,000 new CVs uploaded per week
- 80,000 applications submitted every day
- 6-week listing duration (longer than Indeed's 30 days)
- CV search database
- Multiple pricing models (per-listing, subscription, pay-per-application)
- Established UK-native brand since 1960

### Weaknesses / Gaps vs The Practice Standard
- **Same volume problem as Indeed.** No private practice or healthcare focus. Candidates are not healthcare-screened.
- **Healthcare is an afterthought.** Reed has a separate Reed Healthcare division (as an agency), but Reed.co.uk the job board has no specialist healthcare filtering or candidate verification.
- **Unverified candidates.** No registration or credentials check. Clinical roles attract applications from unrelated sectors.
- **Pricing is higher than it appears.** The headline £89/post figure is for a basic listing with limited visibility. Useful placement costs £199–£799+. Monthly subscription at £100+ is reasonable but the CV database is not healthcare-filtered.
- **Reed Healthcare agency complaints.** Reed consultants have been criticised (Trustpilot, Indeed reviews) for poor communication, sending underqualified candidates, and not following through on commitments.
- **Candidate complaint pattern.** Employers report Reed ghosting, misrepresentation about candidate pools, and duplicate CVs being recycled.
- **Reed rated "Excellent" on Trustpilot (4.3/5)** — primarily driven by candidate (job-seeker) reviews, not employer satisfaction.

### SEO Strength
Very strong. Reed.co.uk is the UK's second-largest job board by traffic, with approximately 3.2M active users monthly. DA is in the 85–90 range. They rank for most job-related healthcare terms, though well below Indeed in raw traffic. Candidate volume is NHS-heavy.

### What Practices Complain About
- Unresponsive consultants who "ghost" after interviews
- Misrepresentation of candidate quality and pools
- Outdated or duplicate CVs in the database
- General job board with no understanding of regulated healthcare professions
- Subscription cost justified only if hiring at volume across all sectors

## 3. NHS Jobs

**URL:** jobs.nhs.uk  
**Type:** Official NHS recruitment platform — government-funded

### Positioning
The NHS's own hiring system. Managed by NHS Business Services Authority. Designed exclusively for NHS trusts and CQC-registered organisations to post clinical and non-clinical NHS roles. Candidates looking for NHS employment — not private practice.

### Pricing
Free for NHS organisations to use. Not commercially accessible to private practices in any meaningful way. The platform requires NHS employer registration through NHS Business Services Authority.

### Key Features
- 30,000+ active NHS listings at any time
- Centralised NHS applicant tracking across NHS trusts
- NHS-standard onboarding (DBS, pre-employment checks)
- Integrated with NHS ESR (Electronic Staff Record)
- Candidate profiles linked to NHS career pathways
- Physiotherapy jobs, nursing, AHP, medical, admin — all NHS only
- Free for NHS orgs; no commercial offering

### Weaknesses / Gaps vs The Practice Standard
- **Completely inaccessible to private practices.** By design. This is not a competitor in commercial terms — it is the explicit foil.
- **Zero relevance to private practice hiring.** A private aesthetic clinic, independent vet practice, or private physio clinic cannot post here.
- **Candidate pool trained for NHS culture.** Professionals on NHS Jobs are specifically seeking NHS roles, NHS pay bands, NHS pension, and NHS career progression. They are not The Practice Standard's candidate.
- **No pricing flexibility, no self-serve, no private practice support.** Heavy, process-driven application system built for large organisations, not nimble private practices.
- **Active positioning tool for The Practice Standard.** NHS Jobs is the "what we are NOT" anchor that sharpens TPS's value proposition. "NHS Jobs is for the NHS. We're for private practice."

### SEO Strength
Enormous. The NHS brand and domain authority (jobs.nhs.uk / nhs.uk) means NHS Jobs dominates all healthcare job searches. However, they're not targeting private practice searches. Professionals actively searching for private roles will NOT go to NHS Jobs — that's the gap.

## 4. Dental Specialist Boards — BDJ Jobs

**URL:** bdjjobs.com  
**Type:** Specialist dental job board  
**Backing:** British Dental Association (BDA) / Springer Nature / British Dental Journal

### Positioning
"The job board for dentists and dental care professionals." The BDA's official recruitment platform. Trusted institutional name. Covers NHS and private dental roles — not exclusively private practice.

### Pricing (2026)
| Package | Non-member | BDA member |
|---|---|---|
| Nurses & Practice Support (30 days) | £155 +VAT | Free |
| Hygienists & Therapists (30 days) | £255 +VAT | £205 +VAT |
| Essential (30 days) | £490 +VAT | £430 +VAT |
| Promoted (40 days) | £690 +VAT | £590 +VAT |
| Premium (60 days) | £790 +VAT | £665 +VAT |
| Institution (senior/academic, 30 days) | £2,075 +VAT | — |

Claimed platform reach: 18,000 monthly site visitors; 735,000+ annual job page views.

### Key Features
- Official BDA association brand — high trust with dentists
- Candidate email alerts to registered dental professionals
- Employer branding (logo, company profile) on listings
- Promoted placement above standard listings
- BDA member discounts (significant — up to £125 off)
- Print advertising in the British Dental Journal available
- Featured advertisers include Bupa Dental Care, PortmanDentex
- Currently: 1,224+ active dental listings

### Weaknesses / Gaps vs The Practice Standard
- **Dental only.** A mixed private practice (dental + aesthetics, or dental + implantology + sleep medicine) cannot consolidate hiring here.
- **No cross-discipline support.** A dental practice that also employs a physiotherapist for TMJ treatment, or an aesthetics practitioner, must use a different board for those roles.
- **Expensive for non-BDA members.** £490–£790 +VAT for a single Essential/Premium listing vs The Practice Standard's £149. Even member pricing (£430+) is 3x TPS pricing.
- **NHS and private mixed.** Candidates cannot filter "private only" — NHS and private roles are intermixed, reducing signal for private practice-specific candidates.
- **Not self-serve without complexity.** Print advertising and tiered packages require more engagement than a simple "post and pay" flow.
- **No coverage of other disciplines.** If a dental group also runs aesthetics, physiotherapy, or optometry — BDJ Jobs is useless for those hires.

### SEO Strength
Moderate-strong within dental. BDJ Jobs ranks well for dental job terms. The BDA brand and BDJ journal backlinks provide solid domain authority for dental-specific searches. Limited to dental namespace.

## 5. Veterinary Specialist Boards — VetClick & Vet Times Jobs

### 5a. VetClick

**URL:** vetclick.com  
**Type:** Specialist veterinary job board and industry hub

### Positioning
"Simple and affordable recruitment services for busy veterinary professionals." UK and international (AU, NZ, SG, USA). Serves vet surgeons, nurses, locums, receptionists, practice managers.

### Pricing
| Type | Cost |
|---|---|
| Single listing | £30 +VAT |
| 10 posts | £140 +VAT |
| 50 posts | £220 +VAT |
| 3-day featured listing boost | £8.24 +VAT |
| 28-day featured boost | £32.98 +VAT |
| Candidate profile access (1 credit) | £25 +VAT |
| **First 3 posts for new employers** | Free |

Platform scale: ~1,881 active jobs; 5,692 registered practices; 192 active job seekers.

### Key Features
- Very low entry price (£30/listing — cheapest of any specialist board)
- Non-expiring credits — buy in bulk, use when needed
- CV/candidate database access via credit system
- Featured listing upgrades for homepage placement
- CPD calendar and industry news hub alongside jobs
- Vet Finder practice directory

### Weaknesses / Gaps vs The Practice Standard
- **Veterinary only.** Multi-discipline private healthcare groups (e.g. a group running vet and aesthetics, or vet and private GP) have no cross-discipline solution here.
- **Small active candidate pool.** 192 active job seekers vs 5,692 practices = very thin candidate-to-employer ratio. Volume is the core weakness.
- **Budget pricing signals cheap, not quality.** The £30/listing price point attracts volume but doesn't position the board as premium. No "quality of hire" narrative.
- **No private practice differentiation.** VetClick covers corporate vet groups (IVC, CVS) and independents alike — no filtering or positioning around independent practice.
- **SEO reach limited to vet namespace.** Won't help a vet-adjacent practice find an aesthetics nurse or physio.

- **No private practice filter.** NHS veterinary, charity/rescue, and corporate group roles sit alongside independent private practice roles.

## 6. Aesthetics Specialist Boards

### 6a. aestheticjobs.uk

**URL:** aestheticjobs.uk  
**Type:** Specialist UK medical aesthetics job board

### Positioning
"UK medical aesthetics jobs — direct hiring for aesthetic clinics." Built for UK aesthetic practitioners: nurse injectors, aesthetic doctors, dentists in aesthetics, aestheticians, clinic managers, front-of-house. Every listing posted by the hiring clinic directly — no agencies, no duplicates.

### Pricing
Pricing not publicly disclosed — the employer portal (aestheticjobs.uk/employers) returned a 403. Based on search data, the model appears to be self-serve pay-per-post, with free candidate access. Exact figures are not publicly available at time of research.

### Key Features
- Direct hiring only — no agency listings
- Built specifically for UK medical aesthetics
- Free for candidates
- Role types covered: clinical (nurse injectors, aesthetic doctors) and non-clinical (clinic managers, front desk)
- Small, focused platform

### Weaknesses / Gaps vs The Practice Standard
- **Aesthetics only.** A clinic that also employs physiotherapists (increasingly common in premium clinics combining aesthetics and body work), dental associates, or private GPs has no solution here.
- **Very small platform.** No public stats on active jobs or registered users. Low brand visibility in search — the domain ranks poorly vs Indeed, Reed, and even general healthcare boards for aesthetic-related searches.
- **No multi-discipline reach.** The aesthetic sector increasingly overlaps with medispa, private GP prescribing, and minor surgery — none of which aestheticjobs.uk covers.
- **Niche within a niche.** Useful for pure aesthetics hiring but not a strategic platform for any practice with mixed services.

### SEO Strength
Weak. The domain does not appear prominently in searches for "aesthetic nurse jobs UK," "aesthetic clinic jobs," or related terms. LinkedIn, Indeed, and Glassdoor dominate those SERPs. This is an opportunity: The Practice Standard's programmatic SEO across aesthetics roles and UK regions should outrank aestheticjobs.uk in most searches.

## 9. Opportunities for The Practice Standard

### 9.1 The Multi-Discipline Gap Is Uncontested

Every specialist competitor covers exactly one discipline. BDJ Jobs = dental only. VetClick = vet only. CSP = physio only. aestheticjobs.uk = aesthetics only. No board exists that allows a private practice to post all its roles — clinical and support — across multiple disciplines in one place. This is the fundamental gap The Practice Standard was built to fill, and no competitor is positioned to close it.

**Tactical implication:** Lead all employer messaging with "post all your roles, not just your dental/vet/physio roles." The multi-site group operator and the diversified clinic (e.g. aesthetics + physio, or dental + implants + facial aesthetics) are the highest-value early customers because every other platform fails them.

### 9.2 Pricing Is Dramatically Favourable

| Competitor | Cost for 30-day private practice listing |
|---|---|
| BDJ Jobs (dental) | £430–£790 +VAT |
| Vet Times Jobs | £595 +VAT |
| CSP Jobs (physio) | £220–£370 +VAT |
| Indeed (sponsored, £25/day) | £700+ for 4 weeks |
| Reed (featured) | £199–£799 +VAT |
| **The Practice Standard** | **£149 +VAT** |

TPS pricing is the lowest of any specialist board by a significant margin, and dramatically below Indeed's real cost of meaningful sponsored reach. At £249/month for Practice Pro (unlimited listings), a multi-site group fills every vacancy cheaper than a single listing on BDJ Jobs.

**Tactical implication:** Use direct price comparison in outreach. "A single dental associate listing on BDJ Jobs costs up to £790. Practice Pro covers all your roles across all disciplines for £249/month."

### 9.3 Candidate Quality Narrative Is Well-Evidenced

Indeed's candidate quality problem in healthcare is extensively documented. Employer Trustpilot reviews confirm: "bombarded with zero-match applicants," "virtually all applications from completely unqualified individuals," "poor site for obtaining skilled healthcare labour (veterinarians specifically)." Reed carries the same problem by volume.

**Tactical implication:** The "68% of Indeed applicants fail initial screening" stat (already in TPS's product context) should be front-and-centre in all practice-facing copywriting. Add the direct quote evidence from Trustpilot reviews. "Your inbox, cleared of noise" is a compelling counter-positioning.

### 9.4 NHS Jobs Is a Reputational Foil

NHS Jobs is free, dominant, and completely irrelevant to private practices. Private practices who try NHS Jobs are not the platform's target, get no traction, and come away frustrated. This is a proven pain point.

**Tactical implication:** In cold outreach and content marketing, explicitly name NHS Jobs as "not for you." "NHS Jobs is built for NHS trusts — not private practices. That's exactly why we built The Practice Standard." This directly addresses the most common "we already use a free board" objection.

### 9.5 SEO Opportunity in Private Practice Intent Searches

While Indeed and Reed have dominant domain authority, they are generic. Their pages for "aesthetic nurse jobs London" or "veterinary surgeon private practice UK" are generic search result pages — not purpose-built content. The Practice Standard's programmatic SEO approach (88 professions × 12 UK regions = 1,144 indexed pages) targets the specific private-practice-intent queries that general boards serve poorly.

Key uncontested searches to target:
- "[profession] private practice jobs [city]" — very few sites target "private practice" specifically
- "private [dental/aesthetic/vet/physio] jobs UK"
- "associate [profession] private practice UK"
- "[profession] jobs no NHS [city]"

VetClick ranks for vet-specific terms but not private-practice-intent vet terms. BDJ Jobs ranks for dental but not "private dental practice jobs." aestheticjobs.uk is barely visible. The CSP board has low listing volume and ranks for few competitive terms.

**Tactical implication:** Build content that explicitly targets the "private practice" intent modifier. Google currently shows a mix of agency sites, LinkedIn, and Indeed for these searches. A well-structured specialist board with proper schema markup and targeted landing pages can take these SERPs without going head-to-head with Indeed on generic terms.

### 9.6 The Aesthetics Sector Is Underserved and SEO-Weak

The dedicated aesthetics boards (aestheticjobs.uk, ARC Aesthetics) have minimal SEO presence. Indeed ranks for "aesthetic nurse jobs UK" but with generic, unfiltered results. LinkedIn shows 19,000+ "aesthetic nurse" roles — but most are completely irrelevant or NHS-adjacent. The aesthetics market (5,000+ independent clinics, 19,700+ botox practitioners) is one of TPS's fastest-growing sectors, and the competition for candidate attention in this space is weak.

**Tactical implication:** The Practice Standard should prioritise aesthetics as a content and SEO beachhead — it's the largest independent clinic category, growing fastest, most underserved by specialist boards, and has the weakest competitor SEO. "Aesthetic jobs" is low-competition compared to "dental jobs" or "physiotherapy jobs."

### 9.7 The Agency Alternative Is Expensive and Hated

Recruitment agencies (ARC Aesthetics, JustPhysio, Dental Elite, countless others) charge 15–25% of first-year salary. On a £40k aesthetic nurse role that's £6,000–£10,000. Practice owners uniformly resent this — it's seen as disproportionate to the value delivered, relationship-dependent, and removing hiring control.

**Tactical implication:** Position TPS's £249/month Practice Pro against agency fees explicitly: "You could fill 5 positions for less than one agency placement." The ROI calculation is not subtle — it's a 95%+ saving per hire. Use this in every outreach, landing page, and pricing page.

### 9.8 Candidate-First Strategy Creates a Flywheel the Specialists Don't Have

The specialist boards (VetClick, BDJ Jobs, CSP) charge practices but don't necessarily serve candidates better than general boards. They have moderate but not exceptional candidate pools (CSP: 5 active listings at research time; VetClick: 192 active job seekers). TPS's "always free for candidates" model, combined with private-practice-specific content and SEO, attracts exactly the professional who wants to move out of the NHS — a candidate that discipline-specific boards don't specifically cultivate.

**Tactical implication:** Content targeting candidates leaving the NHS ("Moving from NHS to private practice: everything you need to know for [profession]") builds candidate supply organically, which in turn makes the platform more valuable to practices — a self-reinforcing flywheel that competitors are not investing in.
