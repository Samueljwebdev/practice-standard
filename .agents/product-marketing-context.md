# Product Marketing Context

*Last updated: 2026-05-24*

## Product Overview
**One-liner:** The UK's specialist job board for regulated private healthcare practices — where better practices hire.

**What it does:** The Practice Standard connects private healthcare practices (aesthetic clinics, veterinary practices, opticians, physiotherapy practices, private GPs, and more) with verified, registered professionals. Practices post roles from £149; professionals always search and apply for free. It's a two-sided marketplace focused exclusively on private practice, not NHS.

**Product category:** Healthcare job board / niche recruitment platform

**Product type:** Two-sided marketplace (SaaS subscription + pay-per-listing)

**Business model:**
- Practice pay: £149 + VAT per listing (30 days) or £249 + VAT/month (Practice Pro — unlimited listings)
- Candidates: always free
- URL: thepracticestandard.co.uk

## Market Context (Why This Platform Is Needed)

The UK private healthcare market is a £13.75 billion industry (LaingBuisson, 2024) projected to reach £18.56 billion by 2033 — growing at 3.4% CAGR. The independent clinics and privately practising doctors segment alone is valued at £5.7 billion.

This growth is being driven by record NHS pressure: the NHS waiting list peaked at 7.7 million patients in late 2023 and remained at 7.1 million as of early 2026, pushing patients and professionals toward private practice. Private GP consultations have risen from 3% of all GP appointments in 2009 to 13% today (LaingBuisson, 2025). There are now 550+ CQC-registered private GP services in the UK.

Despite this scale, **there is no hiring platform built specifically for private practice**. NHS Jobs dominates the NHS. Indeed and Reed serve volume — not quality. Specialist boards (Dentistry.co.uk, RCVS Jobs, GOC jobs) are siloed by discipline. Private practices are left to patch together agency spend, general job boards, and WhatsApp groups.

**Workforce shortage data by discipline:**

- **Optometry**: 10.3% vacancy rate; 21% of weekly workforce is locum-covered; College of Optometrists projects a shortage of ~2,000 practitioners by 2030; only 44% of optometrists work full-time (first time part-timers have taken the lead)
- **Physiotherapy/Allied Health**: 1 physiotherapist per 1,136 people in UK (vs 1 per 742 in Australia); demand grew 7% in 2024; 93% of NHS physio managers report insufficient staff; MSK waiting list exceeded 342,000 referrals in mid-2025
- **Veterinary**: EU registrant numbers dropped 68% between 2019–2021 post-Brexit (1,132 → ~364 annually); pet ownership surge during pandemic drove demand spike; RCVS launched Workforce Action Plan
- **Aesthetics**: 5,000+ independent aesthetic clinics in the UK; 19,700+ practitioners in Botox clinics alone; market value £3.2–3.6 billion with 8.4% annual growth; 65% of practitioners are registered nurses or doctors
- **Private GPs**: £1.6 billion market; 550+ CQC-registered private GP services; 13% of all GP appointments now private (up from 3% in 2009)

## Problems & Pain Points

**Core problem (practices):** Private healthcare practices are invisible on NHS-dominated job boards, and general boards (Indeed, Reed) attract high volumes of irrelevant or unqualified applicants. Specialist agencies charge 15–25% of salary. There is no single platform purpose-built for private practice hiring across all regulated disciplines.

**Core problem (candidates):** Healthcare professionals looking for private sector roles have to search across multiple discipline-specific boards, LinkedIn, and agency listings — there is no single destination for private practice roles across all disciplines.

**Why alternatives fall short:**
- **Indeed / Reed / Totaljobs** — volume boards, no private practice focus, poor candidate quality filtering. 68% of Indeed applicants fail initial screening (industry data). Specialist boards increase application quality by 38% for healthcare roles (Totaljobs data).
- **NHS Jobs** — explicitly NHS-focused, irrelevant to private practices
- **Agencies** — expensive (15–25% of salary), relationship-dependent, not self-serve. Average UK healthcare agency fee: £3,000–£10,000+ per hire depending on role salary.
- **LinkedIn** — not role-specific, cluttered with unrelated content, expensive promoted posts
- **Discipline-specific boards** (e.g., Dentistry.co.uk, RCVS Jobs) — siloed, only cover one profession

**What it costs practices:**
- Agency fees: £3,000–£15,000+ per hire (15–25% of first-year salary; UK average cost-per-hire is £6,125 including internal costs)
- Time-to-fill delays: empty chairs = lost revenue
- Wasted time reviewing unqualified applicants from general boards (68% fail initial screening on Indeed)

**Emotional tension:**
- Practices feel let down by expensive agencies and irrelevant general boards
- Professionals feel like private practice is "invisible" in job search — everything defaults to NHS

## Competitive Landscape

**Direct competitors:**
- **Dentistry.co.uk Jobs** — dental only, no broader private healthcare
- **RCVS Jobs** — veterinary only
- **Aesthetic Jobs UK** — aesthetics only, small community
- **TotallyMed / BMA Jobs** — medical focus, NHS-weighted

**Secondary competitors (different solution, same problem):**
- **Healthcare recruitment agencies** — expensive, relationship-gated, not self-serve. Fee: 15–25% of salary.
- **LinkedIn** — not role-specific, no healthcare filtering, high cost per posting

**Indirect competitors:**
- **Word of mouth / WhatsApp groups** — how many private practices currently hire
- **Indeed** — candidate volume without quality filtering (68% fail initial screening)
- **NHS Jobs** — dominates the market but explicitly wrong for private practice

**How each falls short:**
- Discipline-specific boards require posting on 5–6 different sites to cover all roles
- Agencies remove control and add cost (£3,000–£15,000+ per placement)
- General boards dilute quality and don't understand private practice

## Differentiation

**Key differentiators:**
- Only UK job board covering all regulated private practice disciplines in one place
- Positioning as a quality standard, not a volume board — "high standards attract great people"
- Candidate-first (always free) builds supply naturally
- Programmatic SEO across 88 professions × 12 UK regions = 1,144 indexed pages
- Practice-facing pricing designed to undercut agencies by 90%+

**How we do it differently:** Single destination for all private practice hiring — practices post once, reach professionals across all disciplines. No agency gatekeeping, no NHS noise.

**Why that's better:** Practices get relevant applicants faster and cheaper. Professionals find roles they actually want without sifting through NHS listings.

**Why practices choose us over alternatives:** Cost (fraction of agency fees), quality (registered professionals only), simplicity (self-serve dashboard), and specificity (private practice only — no NHS clutter).

## Switching Dynamics

**Push (away from current approach):**
- Sick of paying agency fees that feel disproportionate to the service received (15–25% of salary)
- Frustrated with Indeed floods of irrelevant CVs from unqualified applicants (68% fail initial screening)
- NHS Jobs is irrelevant; dental/vet-specific boards don't cover all their roles
- WhatsApp hiring feels unprofessional and doesn't scale

**Pull (toward The Practice Standard):**
- The only place to reach registered professionals across all disciplines
- Self-serve — post in minutes, manage from dashboard
- Subscription model enables ongoing hiring without per-placement anxiety
- Feels like it was built for practices like theirs — not adapted from something else

**Habit (keeps them stuck):**
- Existing agency relationships feel safe even when expensive
- "That's just how healthcare hiring works" — industry inertia
- Practice managers don't have time to evaluate alternatives

**Anxiety (about switching):**
- "What if no one applies?" — cold start fear
- "Is this legit / established enough to trust?"
- "We don't have time to manage another platform"

## Brand Voice

**Tone:** Confident, understated, professional — like a well-run practice itself. Not loud or salesy. The quality speaks without shouting.

**Style:** Concise and direct. Short sentences. No jargon. Speaks peer-to-peer with practice owners and clinicians, not down to them. Uses "practices" and "professionals" — never "employers" and "job seekers."

**Personality:** Authoritative, trustworthy, quietly ambitious. The brand believes private practice deserves better infrastructure — and is building it. Not the biggest, but the most relevant.

**Headline approach:** "The hiring standard for modern healthcare" — the brand name is the promise. Everything reinforces the idea that using this platform is itself a signal of operating at a higher standard.
